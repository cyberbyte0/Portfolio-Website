Control & Management of Railway System connected to Microgrid

Published on February 6, 2025 • 10 min read

This project proposes a techno-economic process for energy storage using Supercapacitors (SCs) in trains to reduce energy consumption. The design integrates railway stations with renewable energy sources—specifically Photovoltaic (PV) and Wind systems—along with batteries for the Energy Storage System (ESS).

For the train, SCs are implemented in the ESS, powered during braking phases and from stations via a pantograph installed on the overhead power line at each stop. SCs are selected for their high power characteristics, wide number of charge/discharge cycles, low specific energy, and fast charging time.

The project suggests an energy management approach to control the DC bus by voltage and the buck-boost converter by current. It also details the sizing of PI controllers used for the stabilization of the DC bus for both the train and the station. The entire system is modeled and simulated in MATLAB-Simulink to demonstrate the suitability of the proposed power-train and control strategy.

Key Features

  • Hybrid Energy Storage: Utilization of Supercapacitors (SCs) for trains and Batteries for station ESS.
  • Renewable Integration: Integration of PV and Wind energy sources at railway stations.
  • Regenerative Braking: Harvesting energy during braking phases to charge SCs.
  • Control Strategy:
    • DC Bus Voltage Control.
    • Current Control for Buck-Boost Converters.
    • PI Controller implementation for stability.
  • Techno-Economic Analysis: Focus on reducing overall energy consumption.

Project Structure & MATLAB Files

The core simulations are located in the repository. The files correspond to specific figures and scenarios from the study:

  • Simulation Models (.slx):
    • fig7a12.slx / fig7a12nn_.slx: Likely represents the simulation results for Figures 7, 12, or related scenarios (Standard vs Neural Network/Enhanced variants).
    • fig8a.slx / fig8ann_.slx: Simulation model for Figure 8 scenarios.
    • fig9am.slx / fig9amnn_.slx: Simulation model for Figure 9 scenarios.
  • Data Files (.mat):
    • inputparame.mat: Contains input parameters for the simulation (e.g., irradiation, wind speed, load profiles).
    • mainparamee.mat: Stores main system parameters (e.g., component sizing, controller gains).

Getting Started

To explore the simulations:

  1. Clone the Repository: git clone https://github.com/cyberbyte0/Academic-Project
  2. Setup MATLAB Workspace: Open MATLAB, navigate to the MATLAB FILES directory, and load the required parameter files (inputparame.mat, mainparamee.mat).
  3. Run Simulations: Open a model file (e.g., fig7a12.slx) and click Run in Simulink.
